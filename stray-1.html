I · night walk

Luna crosses the avenue

A winter night in a city you don’t fully know yet. Streetlights tremble in puddles. Something moves between parked cars — a thin shape, a tail, two bright points of light.

You watch from the bus stop. A dog steps off the sidewalk, nose close to the asphalt. She pauses, listening, then slips between the flow of cars as if she’s memorized their rhythm.

People around you react in fragments: someone clicks their tongue, someone turns away, someone whispers, “She’s been here for weeks.”

No collar. No leash. No one calling her name.

Tonight, you decide how this story goes.
As Luna disappears into the dark, you decide not to look away.
