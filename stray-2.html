II · the unseen city

Where Luna walks, the city changes

You follow her tracks deeper into the unlit grid. The air grows colder. The city grows quieter. And the problems grow visible.

Every city hides another version of itself. The one in daylight, and the one at the edge of lamppost shadows.

Luna moves through alleys that don’t exist on tourist maps — empty lots, drainage ditches, loading docks.

And as you follow, you begin to notice patterns: the smell of stagnant water, clusters of food scraps, deserted construction sites — places where strays gather.

A city’s animal population isn’t random. It’s a symptom chart.

The city feels heavier now. You understand her world a little more.
