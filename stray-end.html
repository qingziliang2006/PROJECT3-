The city keeps walking

Laws don’t walk the streets. People do. The story ends here for now — but the city doesn’t.

Somewhere in the same grid of streets, another dog is crossing the avenue. Another person is watching from a bus stop, wondering if they should care.

Your version of the law is only one of many possible drafts. If you changed one clause, one priority, one assumption — the city would shift in another direction.
