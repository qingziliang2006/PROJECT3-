III · rewriting the city

If the law listened to Luna

The city is not broken. It is unfinished. And tonight, you decide how it will be rewritten.

In One Health, cities are organisms — networks of human, animal, and environmental wellbeing.

When one part collapses, the others bend under the weight. But solutions never belong to a single actor. They belong to everyone.

Humans

Health systems, transport safety, mental wellbeing — all intertwined with how a city treats (or neglects) its animals.

Animals

Stray dogs and cats face hunger, traffic, parasites, disease — consequences of human abandonment and lack of regulation.

Environment

Waste exposure, disrupted food webs, contaminated water systems — cities shape the ecosystems that stray animals navigate.
